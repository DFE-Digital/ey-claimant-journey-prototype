Claim an early years financial incentive payment: guidance for practitioners

This scheme enables nurseries in participating local authorities to offer £1,000 payments to recruit new practitioners.

Complete an existing claim

You should only do this when you have received an email from the Department for Education inviting you to complete the claim your employer started.

When you receive an email, you need to complete the claim by 15 March 2025 to receive the payment.

Start now

How it works

To receive the payment, you must have applied for a job that offers a £1,000 payment.

After you’ve been offered and accepted the job, your nursery will start a claim for the payment on your behalf.

After the nursery has started the claim, you’ll receive an email inviting you to complete the claim on a digital service. To do this, we’ll help you create a GOV.UK One Login account if you do not already have one. This is so that we can verify your identity.

When we have verified your identity, we’ll ask you for a few details including your bank account details.

You’ll receive the incentive payment after you’ve been in the role for 6 months.

Eligibility

You’re eligible for a payment if:

  • you have applied for a job with an incentive attached
  • you have not worked in a permanent role in an early years setting in the 6 months before starting your new role, where you spent most of your time (70% or more) working directly with children
  • the role you’ve applied for is a permanent contract (this applies to part and full-time roles)
  • you’ll spend most of the time (70% or more) in your role working directly with children

You do not need to have any early years qualifications or have worked in a nursery before.

You cannot receive the incentive payment if you:

  • have worked in a permanent role in a nursery in the last 6 months
  • will not be spending most of your time (70% or more) working directly with children in your new role

Apprenticeships

Incentive payments are not available to apprentices, even if you have a permanent contract during your apprenticeship.

You can receive a payment when you move from being an apprentice to an employee. To do this, you must have passed your apprenticeship and been taken on in a permanent role.

How you're paid

The Department for Education will pay the money directly into your bank account. This will happen after you have been in the role for 6 months.

Tax and deductions

Income tax

The Department for Education will pay Income Tax and National Insurance on your payment if your total annual income is less than £50,270.

Universal Credit

If you claim Universal Credit, your £1,000 payment may affect the amount of Universal Credit you receive.

Read more about how changes to your earnings can affect your Universal Credit.

Student loan

Your £1,000 payment will not be affected by any funding you have previously received to study for a college course or apprenticeship.

If you have previously studied at university and have a student loan, your £1,000 payment may affect your repayments.

If you're currently repaying a student loan

If your payslip shows that you’re repaying a student loan, a deduction towards it may come out of your £1,000 payment.

If you have a student loan but are not currently repaying it

If your payment takes you over the amount you must earn to start paying back a student loan, a deduction may go towards repaying it.

Read more about when you start paying back a student loan and what you'll pay.

You may be able to claim any deductions back at the end of the tax year. Read more about how you can claim back student loan deductions.

If you leave before you’ve been in the role for 6 months

You must have stayed in your role for at least 6 months to receive the payment.
