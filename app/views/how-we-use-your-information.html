How we'll process your claim

How we check the information you've provided 

We'll check the information you've given us with the nursery where you currently work.

How we pay you

The Department for Education will pay the money into your bank account. This will happen around 6 weeks after we've confirmed you're still working in your role at 6 months.

Income Tax

The Department for Education will pay Income Tax and National Insurance on your incentive payment if your total annual income is less than £50,270.

Universal Credit

If you claim Universal Credit, your £1000 payment may affect the amount of Universal Credit you receive.

Read more about how changes to your earnings affect your Universal Credit.

Student loan

Your £1000 payment will not be affected by any funding you have previously received to study for a college course or apprenticeship.

If you have previously studied at university, your £1000 payment may affect your student loan repayments.

If you're currently repaying a student loan

If your payslip shows that you’re repaying a student loan, a deduction towards it may come out of your £1000 payment.

You may be able to claim any deductions back at the end of the tax year. Read more about how you can claim back student loan deductions.

If you have a student loan but are not currently repaying it

If your payment takes you over the amount you must earn to start paying back a student loan, a deduction may go towards repaying it.

Read more about when you start paying back a student loan and what you'll pay.

You may be able to claim any deductions back at the end of the tax year. Read more about how you can claim back student loan deductions.
